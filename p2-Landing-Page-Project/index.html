Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone

    Chapter 1 - THE BABY WHO LIVED

    Mr and Mrs Dursley, of number four, Privet Drive, were proud to say that they were perfectly normal, thank you very much. You could not expect them to believe in anything strange or mysterious, for they simply did not bother with such nonsense.

    Mr Dursley was the director of a firm called Grunnings, which made drills. He was a tall, muscular man, with hardly any neck, but a large moustache. Mrs Dursley was thin and fair, with a neck twice as long as the average...

    Chapter 2 - THE VANISHING GLASS

    Nearly ten years had passed since the Dursleys had woken to find their grandson on the doorstep, but Privet Drive had not changed a bit. The sun still rose and illuminated the brass number four on the Dursleys' door. The sunlight streamed into the living room, much as it had when Mr. Dursley had sat watching the fateful news report about the owls. Only the photographs hanging by the fireplace had changed over the years. Ten years ago there had been countless pictures on the walls of a baby who looked like a pink beach ball, wearing bows of every color...

    Chapter 3 - THE LETTERS OF NO ORIGIN

    The escape of the Brazilian Boa Constrictor resulted in Harry's longest detention yet. By the time Harry was allowed out under the stairs, the summer holidays had begun and Dudley had smashed his new video camera, smashed his remote-controlled plane, and knocked Mrs Figg off her feet as she tried to cross the road on her first bike ride.

    Harry was glad that the school year was over, but he couldn't escape Dudley's gang, who came to visit every day. Piers, Dennis, Malcolm, and Gordon were all big and small-minded, but Dudley was the biggest and dumbest of the bunch, and was their leader. They all enjoyed taking part in Dudley's favourite sport, hunting Harry Potter.

    Chapter 4 - THE KEYKEEPER

    Boom! The person outside was banging on the door again. Dudley was woken. He asked, bewildered: - Where did the cannons go off? Behind them, the door creaked, and Mr. Dursley came out, gun in hand - Ah, so that long package you saw him carrying to the island was this gun. He shouted: - Who's there? I warn you, I have a gun. There was silence. Then... RRRR...BANG...
